# Common Tourist Scams — Global Reference Database

This file serves as a reference for Tourism Buddy to pull scam warnings from.
See scripts/scam_database.py for the programmatic version.

## By Region

### Southeast Asia
- Motorbike rental damage (Bali, Thailand, Vietnam)
- Gem/jewelry investment scams (Bangkok)
- Tuk-tuk detour to commission shops (Bangkok, Cambodia)
- "Closed today" temple redirect (Bangkok Grand Palace)
- Taxi meter refusal / inflated rates (Bangkok, Bali)
- Fake travel agencies selling bogus bus/boat tickets
- Full Moon Party theft (Koh Phangan)
- Happy pizza/shake drug setups (Cambodia)
- Jetski damage scam (Phuket)

### Europe
- Friendship bracelet / rose gift scam (Paris, Rome, Barcelona)
- Fake petition / charity collectors (Paris, Rome)
- Pickpocket teams at metro/tourist spots
- Fake police checking wallets (Barcelona, Prague)
- Restaurant bill padding / tourist menu pricing (Venice, Athens, tourist zones)
- "Found ring" scam (Paris)
- Street shell game / three-card monte (everywhere)
- Free walking tour with aggressive tip pressure

### South America
- Express kidnapping (Bogotá, Caracas)
- Fake police checking documents/wallets
- "Mustard/bird poop" distraction robbery (Buenos Aires)
- Unlicensed taxi from airport (Lima, São Paulo)
- Drugged drinks (Bogotá "scopolamine")
- Fake tour guides at Machu Picchu

### Middle East & North Africa
- Aggressive carpet/spice shop touts (Istanbul, Marrakech, Cairo)
- "My uncle's shop" friendly redirect
- Inflated taxi rates from airports
- Overcharging at bazaars (know fair prices before negotiating)
- Henna tattoo surprise charges (Marrakech)
- Camel ride bait-and-switch pricing (Egypt, Morocco)

### East Asia
- Tea ceremony scam (Beijing, Shanghai) — "friendly students" invite you to overpriced tea
- Rickshaw overcharging (Beijing hutongs)
- Fake monk charity scam (various)
- Kabukicho bar tout scam (Tokyo)
- Massage parlor overcharging (various)

### Global
- ATM skimming / DCC conversion scam
- WiFi honeypot data theft
- Currency confusion / counterfeit change
- "Broken" taxi meter
- Fake hotel booking confirmations
- Unsolicited "help" with luggage at stations (then demands payment)
- Free sample → aggressive sales pressure
- Overbooked room → redirect to expensive alternative

## Prevention Rules (Universal)
1. Research destination scams BEFORE arriving
2. Use official transport (apps, hotel-arranged, official taxi stands)
3. Never hand over passport/wallet to strangers
4. If someone approaches YOU, be cautious (legitimate businesses don't need to accost tourists)
5. Use a money belt / hidden pouch for large amounts
6. Keep digital copies of all documents in cloud
7. Share itinerary with someone at home
8. Trust your instincts — if it feels wrong, leave
