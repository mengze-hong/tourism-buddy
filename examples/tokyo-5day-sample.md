## ⚠️ Golden Week Warning
You're traveling during Golden Week (Apr 29 - May 5)! This is Japan's biggest holiday:
- **Everything is crowded** — book ahead for popular restaurants
- **Prices are higher** — hotels cost 30-50% more
- **Some businesses close** — verify hours
- **Upside**: Amazing festival atmosphere and decorations!

## Day 1: Shinjuku & Shibuya — Wednesday, May 1

### 🌅 Morning (9:00 AM - 12:00 PM)

**Meiji Shrine (明治神宮)**
⏰ ~1.5 hours | 💰 Free | 📍 1-1 Yoyogi-Kamizonocho, Shibuya
- Arrive early to beat crowds
- Walk through the massive torii gate in the forest
- If lucky, witness a traditional Shinto wedding
- 📷 Best shot: The main torii gate with morning light filtering through trees

🚶 10 min walk through Harajuku →

**Takeshita Street & Harajuku**
⏰ ~1 hour | 💰 Free (shopping extra) | 📍 Takeshita-dori, Harajuku
- Wild fashion, crepes, unique shops
- Try a Harajuku crepe (~¥500/$3.50)
- People-watching heaven

### 🍽️ Lunch (12:00 - 1:00 PM)

**Fuunji (風雲児)** — Tsukemen (Dipping Ramen)
📍 2 Chome-14-3 Yoyogi, Shibuya | 💰 ¥1,000/$7 per person
- ⭐ One of Tokyo's best tsukemen — thick noodles dipped in rich broth
- Expect 20-30 min queue (worth it!)
- Order from the vending machine outside
- English picture menu available

### ☀️ Afternoon (1:30 PM - 5:30 PM)

**Shibuya Crossing**
⏰ 30 min | 💰 Free | 📍 Shibuya Station, Hachiko Exit
- World's busiest pedestrian crossing
- 📷 Best view: Shibuya Sky observation deck or Starbucks 2F at crossing
- Pet the Hachiko statue!

**Shibuya Sky**
⏰ 1.5 hours | 💰 ¥2,000/$13 | 📍 Shibuya Scramble Square
- Book online in advance! Sells out during Golden Week
- Open-air rooftop at 230m — best city views
- 📷 Go at 4:30 PM for golden hour over Tokyo skyline

🚇 JR Yamanote Line → Shinjuku (5 min)

**Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden**
⏰ 1.5 hours | 💰 ¥500/$3.50 | 📍 11 Naitomachi, Shinjuku
- ⚠️ Closes at 6 PM — arrive by 4:30
- Three beautiful garden styles: Japanese, French, English
- May = perfect weather, green everywhere
- No alcohol allowed inside

### 🌙 Evening (6:00 PM - 10:00 PM)

**Omoide Yokocho (Memory Lane / Piss Alley)**
📍 1 Chome-2 Nishishinjuku | 💰 ¥2,000-3,000/$15-20/person
- Narrow alley of tiny yakitori bars (6-8 seats each)
- Incredible atmosphere — smoky, nostalgic, authentic
- Try: Yakitori, motsu (organ skewers if adventurous), beer
- Cash only! Most places

**Golden Gai**
📍 1 Chome-1 Kabukicho, Shinjuku | 💰 ¥1,000-2,000/$7-13
- 200+ tiny bars in narrow alleys (many seat only 5-6 people)
- Some have cover charges (¥500-1000) — ask before sitting
- Look for bars with English menus or "Tourists Welcome" signs
- Don't take photos without asking — it's considered rude

## Day 2: Tsukiji, Asakusa & Akihabara — Thursday, May 2

### 🌅 Morning (7:00 AM - 12:00 PM)

**Tsukiji Outer Market**
⏰ 2.5 hours | 💰 $20-30 for food crawl | 📍 4 Chome Tsukiji, Chuo City
- Arrive by 7 AM! Stalls start closing by noon
- **Must eat**: Tamago (egg) on a stick ¥100, fresh sashimi, grilled scallops, taiyaki
- This is breakfast + snacking — skip hotel breakfast today!
- 📷 Best: The colorful stalls with steam rising

🚇 Metro to Asakusa (15 min)

**Senso-ji Temple**
⏰ 1.5 hours | 💰 Free | 📍 2 Chome-3-1 Asakusa, Taito
- Tokyo's oldest temple (645 AD!)
- Walk through famous Kaminarimon (Thunder Gate) — 📷 iconic shot
- Nakamise shopping street: snacks + souvenirs
- Try: Melon pan (¥200), ningyo-yaki (¥300)
- Draw an omikuji fortune (¥100) — don't worry if it's bad luck, just tie it up!

### 🍽️ Lunch

**Sometaro (染太郎)** — Okonomiyaki
📍 2 Chome-2-2 Nishiasakusa | 💰 ¥1,200/$8 per person
- Cook-it-yourself okonomiyaki (savory pancake)!
- Fun experience — staff helps you
- Historic building, tatami seating

### ☀️ Afternoon (2:00 PM - 6:00 PM)

**Akihabara (Electric Town)**
⏰ 3 hours | 💰 Free-$$ (shopping) | 📍 Akihabara Station area
- Anime, manga, electronics, gaming paradise
- Visit a multi-floor arcade (try UFO catchers!)
- Experience a themed café (maid café ~¥2,000)
- Don Quijote for quirky souvenirs

### 🌙 Evening

**Conveyor Belt Sushi at Genki Sushi or Sushiro**
💰 ¥150-500/plate ($1-3.50) — budget sushi heaven!
- Order by tablet (English available)
- Total for 2: ~¥4,000-6,000 ($27-40)
- Quality is surprisingly excellent for the price

## 🛡️ Tokyo Scam Alerts
1. 🟡 **Kabukicho "Nigerian" Bar Touts** — Aggressive touts (often Nigerian) try to lure you to overpriced bars with hidden charges ($100+ bills for a drink). NEVER follow touts.
2. 🟡 **Fake Monks** — "Monks" giving you beads/charms then demanding "donations." Real monks don't solicit.
3. 🟢 **Overall**: Tokyo is one of the SAFEST major cities in the world. Violent crime toward tourists is extremely rare.
